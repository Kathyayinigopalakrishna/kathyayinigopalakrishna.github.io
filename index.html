Research Overview

Interdisciplinary research across immunity, genetics, nutrition, and the microbiome

Kathy’s work integrates maternal immunity, microbial genetics, pediatric nutrition through breast milk, and host–microbe biology to understand disease susceptibility in early life, including necrotizing enterocolitis.

I

Immunity

Defining how maternal antibodies and mucosal immune factors influence neonatal immune development and microbial colonization.

G

Genetics

Using microbial genetics to identify the pathways that regulate colonization, adaptation, and virulence in early life.

N

Nutrition

Studying how pediatric nutrition and immune components in breast milk shape the neonatal microbiome and disease risk.

M

Host–Microbe

Investigating host–microbe interactions that determine susceptibility to inflammatory and infectious diseases such as necrotizing enterocolitis.
